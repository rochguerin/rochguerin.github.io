Our load-balancing upgrade to [GEOS-Chem](https://geoschem.github.io) is now officially available in the ["Mergeable at any time"](https://wiki.seas.harvard.edu/geos-chem/index.php?title=GEOS-Chem_no-diff-to-benchmark) section of the [GEOS-Chem model development priorities page](https://wiki.seas.harvard.edu/geos-chem/index.php/GEOS-Chem_model_development_priorities), and should normally be incorporated in the 14.7.1 release. 

This modification of GEOS-Chem's default load distribution solution can improve GEOS-Chem's overall runtime by more than 20% in most configurations and by over 40% in high-resolution configurations.   
This is work done by [Daisy Wang](https://daisy0419.github.io/) and [Jordan Sun](https://jordan-sun.github.io/) in collaboration with [Kunal Agrawal](https://www.cse.wustl.edu/~kunal/) (WashU), [Seb Eastham](https://profiles.imperial.ac.uk/s.eastham) (Imperial College), and myself, and with assistance from [Randall Martin](https://sites.wustl.edu/acag/) (WashU).
A report describing the work is being finalized for eventual publication.
