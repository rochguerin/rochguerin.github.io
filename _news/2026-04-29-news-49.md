The paper [*On the Benefits of Reprofiling - The Multiple Hops Case--Part II*](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2604.24930) co-authored with my student Jiaming Qiu](https://qiujiaming315github.io) is now on [arXiv](https://arxiv.org)

The paper wraps-up Jiaming's thesis that explored when and how changing flows' (deterministic) profiles can help multi-hop packet networks guarantee deadlines more efficiently (with less bandwidth).
The paper is Part II of a two-part sequence, with its prequel, [*On the Benefits of Reprofiling - The Multiple Hops Case--Part I*](https://doi.org/10.1109/TNET.2024.3392030)  having been published in the August 2024 issue of the (then) IEEE/ACM Transactions on Networking.   
This Part II extends the investigation of Part I to FIFO and static priority schedulers for which it also develops effective reprofiling solutions.

The work was supported by NSF grant CNS-2006530 and a gift from Google.
