Observations

  • Addition of QoS extensions to OSPF was not an overly complex task

    • Minor impact to topology database and LSA generation/reception process
    • Some possible improvements, but benefits are unclear

  • LSA processing dominates overall overhead

    • Use of coarse triggering policies and hold-down timers can reduce it substantially
      • Increasing threshold from 10% to 80% yields order of magnitude reduction in cost
      • Impacts tail of load distribution as well

  • Path pre-computation can be beneficial with coarse triggering policies

    • Small performance hit even with large pre-computation periods

  • Bandwidth requirements of LSA are marginal
